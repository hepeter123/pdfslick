Three Compression Levels for Every Need

Not all PDFs are the same, and not all compression needs are the same. That's why PDFSlick offers three distinct compression levels. Low compression prioritizes quality — ideal for archival documents, legal filings, and print-ready materials where every pixel matters. Medium compression strikes the perfect balance between file size and visual quality, making it perfect for business reports, presentations, and documents shared via email. High compression squeezes files down to the smallest possible size, which is perfect for web uploads, form submissions, and situations where reading clarity matters more than perfect image fidelity.

How PDF Compression Works

PDFs can be large for many reasons: high-resolution embedded images, unoptimized fonts, redundant metadata, and inefficient internal data structures. Our PDF compressor addresses all of these factors. It re-encodes embedded images at an optimized quality setting, removes redundant data streams, and optimizes the internal object structure of the PDF. The result is a standards-compliant PDF that's noticeably smaller but visually very similar to the original.

Privacy First — Your Files Never Leave Your Device

One of the biggest concerns with online PDF tools is privacy. When you compress a PDF using PDFSlick, the file is processed entirely within your web browser using JavaScript — specifically the pdf-lib library. Your document data is never transmitted to any server. This makes PDFSlick safe for sensitive documents including financial statements, legal contracts, medical records, and personal identification documents.

Photographers compress PDF portfolios before emailing to agencies. HR professionals compress application packets before forwarding to hiring managers.
