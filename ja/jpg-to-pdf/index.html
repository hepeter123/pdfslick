JPG to PDF Converter — Free, Fast, and Private

Converting JPG images to PDF is one of the most common document tasks, whether you're compiling a photo portfolio, archiving receipts, submitting scanned documents, or assembling a presentation. PDFSlick's free online JPG to PDF converter makes this process effortless, requiring no software installation, no account creation, and no file uploads to any external server.

Why Convert Images to PDF?

PDF (Portable Document Format) is the universal standard for sharing documents because it preserves layout, resolution, and formatting across every device and operating system. When you share a JPG, the recipient sees just a single image; when you share a PDF, you can combine dozens of images into a single, professionally organized file. PDFs are also easier to print at the correct scale, more widely accepted by government offices and businesses, and simpler to secure with passwords or permissions.

Supported Image Formats

Although this tool is commonly called a "JPG to PDF converter," it handles a broad range of image formats. You can convert JPEG, JPG, PNG, WebP, BMP, GIF, and TIFF files to PDF in one go. Each image becomes a separate page in the output PDF, sized to fit the image while maintaining its aspect ratio.

Batch Conversion — Multiple Images at Once

Unlike many tools that only handle one image at a time, PDFSlick lets you upload up to 50 images simultaneously. After uploading, a thumbnail grid lets you drag and drop images into the exact order you want them to appear in the PDF. You can also remove individual images before converting, and add more files at any point before you click Convert.

100% Browser-Based — No Privacy Concerns

Many online PDF converters upload your files to a remote server for processing. This creates serious privacy risks, especially when your images contain sensitive personal or financial information. PDFSlick uses the open-source pdf-lib library, which runs entirely inside your web browser using JavaScript. Your images are never transmitted to any server — they are read directly from your local drive, converted in memory, and the resulting PDF is downloaded straight to your device. The PDFSlick server never sees your files.

Tips for Best Results

For the sharpest PDF output, start with the highest-resolution images you have. If you're converting scanned documents, use at least 300 DPI scans for crisp, readable text. If you need a smaller file size, consider compressing the PDF after conversion using PDFSlick's Compress PDF tool. For a professional touch, you can also merge your converted PDF with other existing PDF documents.
